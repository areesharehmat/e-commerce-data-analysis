# Executive Findings Report: Superstore Data Analysis

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **Total Transactions:** 9,994
- **Total Revenue:** $2,297,200.86
- **Total Profit:** $286,397.02

---

## Business Questions Answered

### 1. What was total revenue?
The total revenue generated across all categories and regions is **$2,297,200.86**.

### 2. What was total profit?
The total net profit achieved is **$286,397.02**.

### 3. Which category is most profitable?
**Technology** is the most profitable category with a total profit of **$145,454.95** (on revenue of $836,154.03).

### 4. Which category is least profitable?
**Furniture** is the least profitable category with a total profit of only **$18,451.27**, despite generating a high volume of revenue ($741,999.80). This indicates severe margin compression.

### 5. Which region is best-performing?
The **West** region is the top-performing region in both revenue (**$725,457.82**) and profit (**$108,418.45**).

### 6. Which state is worst-performing?
**Texas** is the worst-performing state, incurring a substantial net loss of **-$25,729.36**. Other poorly performing states include Ohio (-$16,971.38) and Pennsylvania (-$15,559.96).

### 7. Which discounts hurt profitability?
Any discount level **30% and higher** severely hurts profitability, resulting in an average negative profit per transaction. 
- **0% to 20% discounts** maintain positive average profitability.
- **30% discounts** result in an average loss of **-$45.68** per transaction.
- **50% discounts** lead to the deepest average losses of **-$310.70** per transaction.

### 8. Which customer segment generated the most revenue?
The **Consumer** segment generated the most revenue at **$1,161,401.34** (and also the highest total profit of $134,119.21).

### 9. Which shipping mode was most profitable?
**Standard Class** shipping was the most profitable, contributing **$164,088.79** to total profits, driven by its high usage volume ($1,358,215.74 in sales).

---

## Three Recommendations for Management

1. **Re-evaluate and Limit Aggressive Discounting Strategies:** The data reveals that discounts of 30% or more consistently result in net losses. Management should institute a strict policy cap on promotional discounts, keeping them below 20% unless specifically clearing obsolete inventory. Focus instead on value-added promotions or loyalty incentives that don't destroy margins.

2. **Overhaul the Furniture Category and Eliminate Unprofitable Subcategories:**
   Despite generating over $741k in revenue, Furniture only brought in $18.4k in profit. Looking closer at subcategories, **Tables** (-$17,725.48) and **Bookcases** (-$3,472.56) are major loss leaders. Management should review vendor costs, raise prices on tables, or consider phasing out underperforming product models entirely.

3. **Address Regional Pricing and Strategy Issues in Texas and Ohio:**
   Texas and Ohio are bleeding profits (-$25.7k and -$17.0k respectively), likely due to localized aggressive pricing or high promotional discounts. Management should audit the branch offices or distribution networks in these specific states to align pricing policies with profitable regions like the West.
